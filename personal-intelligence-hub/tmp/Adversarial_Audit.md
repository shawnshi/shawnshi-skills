# Adversarial Audit: L4 Intel Stress-Test (2026-03-20)

## 0. Initial Vulnerability Hypothesis (IVH)
The transition to "Liquid Code" and "Model Medicine" risks decoupling from physical system reliability, creating a "Semantic Hallucination" where the specification is perfect but the execution environment (MicroVM/NixOS) is too brittle for clinical legacy systems.

## 1. Dialectic Engagement

### Assertion A: Model Medicine (Clinical Diagnosis of LLMs)
- **Critique (Red Team)**: Treating models as biological entities is a category error. Models are statistical probability maps. "Diagnosing" a symptom (e.g., hallucination) without understanding the underlying gradient landscape is equivalent to medieval humors theory.
- **Defense (Mentat)**: Clinical medicine itself is an empirical science of complex systems where the internal mechanism (e.g., consciousness, drug interaction) is often a black box. The "symptom-to-intervention" loop (CORE-Acu) is a pragmatic framework to ensure safety regardless of whether the model is "biological" or "statistical."
- **Steelmen Recommendation**: The "Model Diagnosis" must be mapped to specific **Linear Probing** or **Activation Steering** interventions, turning the metaphor into a control theory.

### Assertion B: Hot-Pool MicroVM Isolation
- **Critique (Red Team)**: Hospital IT staff cannot maintain NixOS/MicroVM layers. The "Cold Start" latency of VMs, even if milliseconds, adds jitter to high-frequency clinical interactions.
- **Defense (Mentat)**: The cost of a security breach or an unverified "liquid code" mutation in a hospital is catastrophic. NixOS provides "Immutable Declarative State," which is easier to audit than a mutable Windows/Linux server.
- **Steelmen Recommendation**: Implement **MicroVM Pooling** (Pre-warmed instances) and a "Nix-to-Standard" deployment bridge to lower the operational barrier.

### Assertion C: Liquid Code & MSL Sovereignty
- **Critique (Red Team)**: If the "Source Code" is a consumable, the "Evidence-Mesh" becomes the single point of failure. If the test suite (the Mesh) has a bug, the entire system evolves into a coherent but incorrect state.
- **Defense (Mentat)**: This is why MSL must be a **Formal Specification** (Hard-Lock), not just a collection of tests.
- **Steelmen Recommendation**: MSL must incorporate **Formal Verification (TLA+/Coq)** for core clinical constraints, ensuring the "Liquid Code" refactoring stays within the "Safe Corridor."
